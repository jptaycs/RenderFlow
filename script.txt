Here is how it works. The panels on the roof make electricity. That electricity runs into a big box called an inverter which changes it into the kind of power your house uses. And then, this is the important part, it connects to the power company's wires. The electricity your panels make flows out onto the grid and the power company keeps track of it and gives you a credit. Sounds fine. Here is the problem. In the summer when the sun is high and your panels make more than you use, the extra flows out to the power company. They credit you for it, but often at their cheap wholesale price. Then in the winter when the sun is low and your panels make less than you need, you pull power back from the grid and they charge you for it at their expensive retail price. You sold low and you buy back high and the difference goes into their pocket. They call it net metering. Your neighbor called it being told he was independent and then getting a bill anyway.
